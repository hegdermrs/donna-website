Some days you run the business. Most days the business runs you — call to call, email to email, fire to fire. Donna puts you back on the right side of that.

What can Donna do?

Day one

She reads everything.

Years of your email, in about an hour — starting with the mail you sent, because that’s your voice and your people. By the time you’ve made coffee, she knows who matters, what’s owed, and what’s overdue.

The first week

She takes the inbox and the calendar.

A short brief every morning — what needs you, what she’s already handled. Replies drafted in your voice, waiting on your yes. And before every meeting, one page on who you’re seeing and where things stand.

Week three

She starts catching things.

The renewal that quietly went up. The promise about to slip. The invoice nobody chased. She watches the patterns in your business, so surprises come from her — with time to act — not from the bank statement.

From then on

She takes on more — at your pace.

New kinds of work start as drafts for your approval. Once she’s done something right several times in a row, she asks to handle it on her own. You say yes, or you don’t. Either way, everything she does is checked and written down.

She lives on your computer.

Not on a website, not on somebody’s server — on the machine in front of you. Her memory of your business sits in a file you own, on a disk you own. Nothing about your business is sold, shared, or used to train anything.

She never forgets.

Every person, promise, number, and date she reads becomes memory she keeps. Ask about “the March invoice” a year from now and she knows exactly which one you mean — because she was there.

There are AI agents. And there’s Donna.

Plenty of tools can act on a computer. Most are built for people who love setting things up. She’s built for people who have a business to run.
