Every owner ends up doing two jobs.

The business, and the busywork around it — email, scheduling, chasing invoices, remembering who was promised what. Donna takes the second one.

How it works

You started a business to do the thing you’re great at. Then, quietly, a second job moved in: answering the email, juggling the calendar, chasing the invoice, remembering that you promised Dana an answer by Friday. Nobody hired you for that job. You just became the person who does it.

The cost isn’t the hours — though it’s the hours too. It’s what the job does to your attention. The renewal that went up in March, found out from the charge. The good client who’d gone quiet for three weeks before anyone noticed. The deal that didn’t fall through so much as fade, one unsent follow-up at a time. When you’re the assistant and the owner, the owner is the one who slips.

So we gave the job to someone else.

Her name is Donna. She lives on your computer — not on a website, not on somebody’s server — and she works the way a great assistant works: she’s assertive, so you get “the Rivera invoice is 12 days late — want it chased?” instead of a hint. She’s on top of it, so the brief lands at 7:00 and the inbox is sorted before you’re up. She takes initiative, so the draft exists before you asked. And she sees what you don’t — the dates, the patterns, the silences.

Her first day sets the tone.

The morning you sign in, she reads years of your email in about an hour — your sent mail first, because that’s your voice and your people. By evening she knows who matters, what’s owed, and what’s overdue. Within a week she’s running the inbox and the calendar: drafts in your voice waiting on a yes, one page in your hand before every meeting. By week three, the surprises start coming from her — early, while there’s still time to act — instead of from the bank statement.

And she earns her way forward. New kinds of work start as drafts for your approval; only after she’s been right again and again does she ask to handle something on her own. Everything she does is checked, written down, and undoable. You stay the owner. She stays the assistant. That’s the whole arrangement.
